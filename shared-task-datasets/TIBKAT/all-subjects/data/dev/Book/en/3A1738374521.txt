Object design style guide
Well-written OO code is a pleasure to read, modify, and debug.  Elevate your coding style by mastering the universal best practices for object design presented in this book.  These clearly presented rules, which apply to any OO language, maximize the clarity and durability of your codebase and increase productivity for you and your team.  "Object design style guide" presents dozens of professional techniques for writing OO code.  In it, veteran developer Matthias Noback lays out design rules for constructing objects, defining methods, changing and exposing state, and much more.  All example use instantly familiar pseudocode, so you can follow along in the language you prefer.  You'll go case by case as you explore important scenarios and challenges for object design and then walk through a simple web application that demonstrates how different types of objects can work together effectively
