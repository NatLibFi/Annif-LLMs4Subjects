Finding Problems to Fit the Solutions : Twenty Years of Aid to the Sahel
International development cooperation is in crisis. Questions abound: is it effective? Is it even useful? After ten years of &quot;aid fatigue&quot;, here is a lucid, constructive book that sheds new light on the problems, and makes proposals for reform that are both thoughtful and innovative. Jean-David Naudet's analysis is a genuine contribution to the literature on aid. He combines the insight of the sociologist and the experience of the practitioner, with a few enlightening incursions into organization theory.
