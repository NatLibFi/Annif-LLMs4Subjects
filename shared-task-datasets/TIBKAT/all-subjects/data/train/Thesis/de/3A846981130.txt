Limitations in global information on species occurrences

Detailed information on species distributions is crucial to answering central questions in ecology, evolutionary biology and biogeography and for effectively allocating conservation resources among regions. Huge numbers of species occurrence records, the basic data underlying our knowledge of species distributions, have been mobilized via international data-sharing networks, most notably that of the Global Biodiversity Information Facility (GBIF).
