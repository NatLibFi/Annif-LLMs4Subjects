The olfaction of insect species is adapted to the requirements of the specific ecosystem they are living in. Volatiles perceived by insects can transport information on the location of an oviposition place or the physiological status of a host plant. As the olfaction of insects has adapted to the perception of distinct volatiles since millions of years, these volatiles can be regarded as marker compounds, which enable a reliable assessment of an ecological status.
