The supply of short-lived deciduous trees (ALn) in Lower Saxony was analysed using data from the German national forest inventory (NFI, 2002 and 2012). The standard results of the NFI were augmented by own calculations, which allowed ALn species to be looked at individually. In terms of both standing volume and area covered, birch and alder, with 80 %, make up the major share of the ALn in Lower Saxony.
