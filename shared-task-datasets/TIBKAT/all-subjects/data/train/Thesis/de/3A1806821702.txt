Global change drivers such as climate change, land-use change, anthropogenic N deposition, deteriorating P nutrition and other calamities jeopardize temperate forest ecosystem functions. Essential components for the maintenance of ecosystem functions are fine roots and soil microbial communities because they are important for nutrient provision and water uptake of trees.
