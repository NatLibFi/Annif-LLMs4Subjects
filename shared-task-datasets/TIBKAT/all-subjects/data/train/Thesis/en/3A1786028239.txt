Impact of tropical rainforest transformation in Indonesia on soil carbon sources, turnover and losses
Agricultural expansion is one of the most dominant drivers of deforestation word wide. The globally rising demand for cash crops such as oil palm and rubber, caused rapid agricultural expansion during the last decades, especially in South East Asia. Conversion of tropical rainforest to monocultural plantations are expected to lead to severe alterations in biogeochemical cycling and ecosystem functioning.
