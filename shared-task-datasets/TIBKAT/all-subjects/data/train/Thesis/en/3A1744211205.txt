Functional interrelations of governance elements and their effects on tropical deforestation - combining qualitative and quantitative approaches
The global rate of annual net forest loss has slowed from 7.8 mio hectares in the 1990s to 4.7 mio hectares between 2015 and 2020. Nevertheless, the area of the world’s forests continues to decrease specifically in the tropics. Improved governance has come into the focus as a means to help reversing trends of tropical deforestation. Yet, “good governance” remains a normative, broad and often unspecified concept consisting of a wide range of elements and implicit value judgements.
