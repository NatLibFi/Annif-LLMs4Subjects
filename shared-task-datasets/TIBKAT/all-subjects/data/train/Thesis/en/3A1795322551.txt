Influence of competition and other factors on crown and stem characteristics of northern red oak
Native to northeastern America and southeastern Canada, northern red oak (Q. rubra L.) was introduced to Europe in 1691 and initially planted in parks and gardens. In the following centuries, three silvicultural cultivation periods in Europe demonstrated that northern red oak had both good growth and favorable silvicultural treatment options. The cultivation of native tree species, however, was always favored.
