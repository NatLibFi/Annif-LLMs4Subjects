Verzerrter Recall als potentielles Hindernis für Synergie bei Gruppenentscheidungen
In hidden profile decision-making tasks, groups often fall short of their potential to make a better decision than each individual group member. Several reasons for this phenomenon have already been established, such as biases regarding both the content of the group discussion and the individual evaluation of information relevant to the decision. This thesis looks into a further aspect of individual information processing where biases could be detrimental to the quality of group decisions: The individual recall of information relevant to the task at hand.
