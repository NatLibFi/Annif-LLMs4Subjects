Trust and reciprocity in the market-based provision of public goods : experimental evidence and applications to conservation tenders
Payments for ecosystem services (PES) and their allocation via competitive tendering processes is an important and innovative instrument in the battle against on-going biodiversity loss. Based on a contractual arrangement between a service provider and a private or public entity willing to buy the conservation service, favourable land management activities or extensive agricultural practices can be stipulated.
