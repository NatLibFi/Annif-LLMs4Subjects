Re-evaluating the influence of preferences on attraction and relationship decisions
Several theories assume that humans possess partner preferences influencing their selection of a partner. It has been proposed that humans seek a partner meeting their preferences and that a match between their preferences and a partner’s characteristics influences a relationship’s well-being. However, the evidence supporting this hypothesis is mixed, which challenges the idea that preferences guide decisions regarding the relationship (starting from preferences influencing decisions of initial attraction up to established relationships).
