Heterochromatisches Gensilencing und die Kontrolle der Pathogenität von C. graminicola
Colletotrichum graminicola; Pathogenität; Epigenetik; Heterochromatin; Histonmethylierung; DNA-Methylierung; Heterochromatin Protein 1 (HP1)
The filamentous ascomycete Colletotrichum graminicola is the causative agent of anthracnose leaf spot and stalk rot on corn. Epigenetic regulatory mechanisms for the formation of heterochromatin, such as H3K9 trimethylation (H3K9me3) or DNA methylation, have been poorly understood in this organism. Targeted knockout analyzes of putative histone and DNA methyltransferase genes reveal that these factors play a crucial role in the formation of heterochromatin in C. graminicola. The heterochromatin protein 1 (HP1) acts as an adapter between H3K9me3 and DNA methylation. Furthermore, it has been shown that this heterochromatin protein 1 is essential for the pathogenicity of the fungus.
