The isotopic composition of CO2 and H2Ov fluxes in a managed beech forest : Instrument tests and ecological application of two laser-based absorption spectrometers
Stable isotopes provide valuable insights into the coupled carbon dioxide (CO2) and water vapor (H2Ov) cycle on scales ranging from one leaf to global. In particular for applications under field conditions, recent progress in laser-based measurement techniques enhanced the potential of stable isotope research to investigate CO2 and water vapor (H2Ov) exchange processes. Here we present two experiments which use two laser-based infrared spectrometers and two ecosystem-scale methods to measure isotopic compositions of different parts of the CO2 and H2Ov cycle.
