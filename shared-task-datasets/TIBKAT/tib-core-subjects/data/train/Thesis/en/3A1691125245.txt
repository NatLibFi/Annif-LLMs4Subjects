Kupfer-katalysierte Azid-Alkin-Cycloaddition zur Modifikation von Kohlenhydraten
The copper-catalyzed azide-alkyne cycloaddition (CuAAC) is one of the most prominent representatives of click chemistry. Due to its facile procedure and high yields it has been growing in popularity since its discovery 17 years ago. The reaction can be performed in the presence of water and air, but catalyst systems such as copper(I)-iodide/N,N-diisopropylethylamine (DIPEA) also allow the reaction to be performed under inert atmosphere and moisture-free conditions.
