Genetic analyses to determine the genetic variation and region of origin of introduced northern red oak (Quercus rubra L.) populations
Northern red oak (Quercus rubra L.) has its natural distribution area in the eastern and north-eastern USA and south-eastern Canada and was first introduced at the end of the 17th century in France. Later, Q. rubra was planted in parks and botanical gardens all over Europe. In comparison to native white oak species, northern red oak can be harvested after only 80−120 years and features a comparably low demand for water and nutrients.
