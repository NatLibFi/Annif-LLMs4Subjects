Integrating remotely sensed data into forest resource inventories : the impact of model and variable selection on estimates of precision
The past two decades have demonstrated a great potential for airborne Light Detection and Ranging (LiDAR) data to improve the efficiency of forest resource inventories (FRIs). In order to make efficient use of LiDAR data in FRIs, the data need to be related to observations taken in the field. Various modeling techniques are available that enable a data analyst to establish a link between the two data sources.
