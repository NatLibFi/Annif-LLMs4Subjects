The northern Mongolian mountain forest steppe, the transition zone between taiga and open steppe, is rich in plant and animal life. It is the most productive region in the country in terms of agriculture and, as a result, exhibits settlement density above average for Mongolia. Light taiga stands, dominated by larch (Larix sibirica Ledeb.), are the backbone of Mongolian production forests and become increasingly opened to the growing demand for timber and firewood.
