Wood anatomy and cytokinin-related responses in poplar (Populus sp.) under environmental stress
Woody plants like poplar are of great importance as a second generation bioenergy crop. However, wood formation is dynamic and strongly affected by exogenous factors such as drought or seasonality, and endogenous factors. Drought negatively affects wood growth and results in significant changes in wood anatomy in poplar. But the intraspecific variations in drought-induced wood anatomical changes and the underlying molecular responses are not clear.
