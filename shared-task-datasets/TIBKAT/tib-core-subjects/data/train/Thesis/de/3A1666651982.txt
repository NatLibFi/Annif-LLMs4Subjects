Due to its physical properties Biochar has the potential of stabilizing a forest rejuvenation during periods of drought. To verify this, a field trial was set up in 2014 in the Hessian Ried. The observed mortality of the trees should be significantly lower compared to the local method. The site is characterized by sandy soil and its low water holding capacity and a low nitrogen supply. The pH is usually above 6.2 and is due to the calcareous substrate. In a randomized block trial, six different coal variants with 80 t ha-1 were applied and milled to a soil depth of 25 cm.
