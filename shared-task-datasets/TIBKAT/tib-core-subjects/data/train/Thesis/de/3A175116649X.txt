Modern arable agriculture faces major challenges. The increasing occurrence of heat waves and long-lasting droughts leads to excessive loss of soil water, to increased risk for wind and water erosion of soils, and subsequent yield losses. Agricultural practices therefore have to be rethought. Agroforestry (AF), a combination of perennial trees and grassland or annual rotating crops, have been shown to increase the soil fertility and the sequestration of carbon dioxide in standing biomass and soil, reduce nutrient leaching into the ground water, and most important, alter the microclimate.
