Ecojustice education : toward diverse, democratic, and sustainable communities
"EcoJustice Education offers a powerful model for cultural ecological analysis and a pedagogy of responsibility. Martusewicz, Edmundson, and Lupinacci provide teachers and teacher educators with the information and classroom practices they need to help develop citizens who are prepared to support and achieve diverse, democratic, and sustainable societies in an increasingly globalized world. Updated with cutting-edge research, statistics and current events throughout, the third edition addresses important topics such as indigenous learning, Black Lives Matter, the Flint Water Crisis, Standing Rock, the rise of fascism, and develops EcoJustice approaches to confronting these issues"--
